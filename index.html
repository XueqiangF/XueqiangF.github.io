Brief Bio

Xue-Qiang Fan is currently a Ph.D. Candidate major in School of Computer and Information, Information and Communication Engineering, Hefei University of Technology, supervised by Prof. Zhong-Yi Guo. Before that, I received M.S. degree in Control Science and Engineering from College of Information Engineering, Zhejiang University of Technology, Hangzhou, China, in 2022, supervised by Prof. Jun Hu, and received B.S. degree in Automation from Henan University of Urban Construction, Pingdingshan, China, in 2019.

Currently, my primary research interest is to design effective visual understanding models for visual systems, involving Artificial Intelligence, Pattern Recognition, Polarization Vision, and Data Mining.

In particular, my recent endeavor is on Polarization Scattering Imaging (PSI), which aims to reconstruct the objects hidden inside scattering media (e.g., ground glass, turbid water, and haze) from a speckle pattern-carrying polarization information. Meanwhile, I also have been carrying on scene confusing-discovery research, which aims to mine the camouflaged/confusing/weak/special yet meaningful object/region in the scene by using Polarization Information. These objects are very common in daily life scenes but can confuse the vision systems due to their inherently special properties. I am pursuing exploring useful cues based on Polarization and effective methods for accurate detection.

In addition, my early work was on Bioinformatics, with a special focus on the identification of biochemical properties and functions related to Proteins, RNA and DNA, (e.g., Identification of DNA N6-Methyladenine Sites) which are the cornerstone of providing more detailed information for Protein Drug Design and Molecular Diagnostics.
